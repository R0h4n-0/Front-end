MY NEIGHBOUR TOTORO




Description:


Release Date: April 16, 1988
Director: Hayao Miyazaki
Screenplay: Hayao Miyazaki




The film begins with Ponyo, a curious and adventurous fish, living in the sea with her father, Fujimoto, a sorcerer who has renounced the human world. Ponyo longs to explore the world beyond the ocean and often sneaks away from her father's watchful eye to do so. One day, while venturing out to sea, Ponyo becomes trapped in a glass jar and is rescued by Sosuke, a five-year-old boy who lives in a small seaside village with his mother, Lisa, a nursing home operator, and his father, Koichi, a sailor. Sosuke is delighted to have found a new friend and names the fish "Ponyo," promising to take care of her. He brings her home and keeps her in a bucket of water. Over time, Ponyo grows fond of Sosuke and decides she wants to become human so she can be with him forever. In her eagerness to transform, she accidentally spills magical elixirs into the sea, causing a chain reaction that threatens to upset the balance of nature. Meanwhile, Fujimoto discovers that his daughter is missing and sets out to find her. He realizes that Ponyo has become infatuated with the human world and is determined to stop her from becoming human at any cost. He calls forth a powerful storm to force her to return to the sea, but Ponyo has already tasted human blood and has become too powerful to control. As Ponyo's transformation progresses, her magical powers cause the ocean to rise and waves to flood the village. Sosuke and his mother are stranded in their house, while Ponyo's love for Sosuke gives her the strength to break free from her father's hold and become fully human. She returns to Sosuke, and they set out on a journey to find Lisa, who has gone to work to help care for the elderly during the storm. Along the way, they encounter various magical creatures, including a giant sea goddess and a school of fish who guide them on their journey. Fujimoto, who has realized the error of his ways, joins them on their quest to find Lisa. They eventually reach Lisa's workplace, where they are reunited and escape the storm together. As the storm clears, Ponyo and Sosuke bid farewell to their magical friends and return home, where they are greeted by their relieved parents. Ponyo, now fully human, must adjust to life on land and learn to live as a human girl. The film ends with Ponyo and Sosuke running off to explore their new world together.
